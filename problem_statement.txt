PROBLEM STATEMENT 04
AI-Powered Autonomous Drone Response for
Urban Safety
Domain: AI 
Background 
As cities grow in scale and complexity, ensuring public safety in real time has
become a major challenge. Traditional surveillance systems are passive and rely
heavily on manual monitoring, which leads to delayed responses during
emergencies such as public disturbances, accidents, unauthorized intrusions,
and suspicious activities.
With the evolution of Artificial Intelligence, real-time video analytics, geospatial
intelligence, and autonomous aerial systems, it is now possible to build proactive
safety platforms that can: • Detect incidents automatically • Dispatch the nearest
responder • Provide live situational awareness to control centre This hackathon
challenges participants to design a simulation-based AI-powered aerial urban
safety system that improves emergency response time and enables smarter
city-wide monitoring.Problem Statement 
Build an AI-powered urban safety platform that: 
• Detects safety incidents from video feeds using computer vision 
• Associates detected incidents with a geolocation (simulated or mapped) 
• Automatically dispatches the nearest available drone (simulated) 
• Displays real-time drone movement on a map-based command dashboard 
• Provides live incident monitoring and status updates The solution must
demonstrate a complete working flow from: Video Input → AI Detection → Alert
Generation → Drone Dispatch → Live Map Visualization Physical drones are not
required. All drone behaviour and movement can be simulated in software.

• Unauthorized entry into restricted zones 
• Suspicious vehicle stops 
• Abandoned objects 
• Traffic rule violations (optional) 
Upon detection, the system should: 
 Trigger an alert 
Highlight the incident on the map 
Display incident details (type, time, location, source feed) 
Note: Face recognition is not required and should not be implemented.
Drone Movement Visualization (Mandatory) 
The system must show real-time drone movement, including: 
• Current position updates 
• Route from drone to incident location
